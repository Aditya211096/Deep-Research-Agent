# Socioeconomic Synthesis (India Focus)

All forensic audits must translate corporate filings and macro policies into their real-world impact on the citizens of India:

## 1. Employment and Job Quality
- Forecast the job creation capacity (direct and indirect) using verified multipliers.
- Scrutinize the quality and stability of the jobs generated (e.g. gig economy delivery jobs with high churn vs. stable manufacturing and engineering roles).
- Flag lay-off patterns or founder-led restructuring actions occurring immediately before pre-IPO filings.

## 2. Cost of Living and Pricing Squeeze
- Evaluate how corporate pricing power, GST changes, or new tariff walls affect consumer purchase power.
- Audit real estate and land price appreciation around mega-projects; evaluate whether it drives localized inflation that pushes out the local population.
- Assess how utility pricing (e.g. airport user development fees, toll taxes, electricity tariffs) impacts daily travel or household budgets.

## 3. Consumer Welfare & Safety
- Scan for shortcuts taken in consumer safety, product quality, or worker protection to meet aggressive cost targets.
- Highlight community protests or litigation resulting from land acquisition disputes, environmental degradation, or corporate governance failures.
