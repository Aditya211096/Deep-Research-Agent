# Forensic Case Study & Editorial Synthesis Compiler

To create an unassailable summary that is extremely readable and easy to remember, you must synthesize the 5 research tracks into a cohesive editorial layout matching the following structured sections:

## 1. Bold, Editorial Headline
- Generate a single bold, punchy, cynical, or ironic headline (max 15 words) that summarizes the core failure or contradiction. E.g. "Authorities have made such a joke of new Jewar airport." or "InCred Holdings: The Premium Valuation Game vs Footnote Vulnerabilities."

## 2. The Promise (Numbered List)
- Deconstruct the initial narrative or promotional plan.
- Format this strictly as a simple, high-density numbered list matching the sample layout:
  "The promise of [Subject] was simple [N] things:
  1 - [Promise 1]
  2 - [Promise 2]
  3 - [Promise 3]"
- Keep each item short, direct, and under 15 words.

## 3. The Reality Deconstruction
- Summarize the core audited reality that refutes or qualifies the promises.
- Limit this to 1-2 concise, high-density sentences (max 40 words).

## 4. Numerical Variance Matrix (Side-by-Side Comparison)
- Extract 2-4 critical financial or operational metrics.
- Provide a clear side-by-side comparison between the standard/baseline value (e.g. competitors, older hubs) and the target value discovered in footnotes/prospectuses, with a calculated mismatch or variance percentage.

## 5. Multi-Lens Implications
- **Operating Lens (Business Implications)**: Output exactly 2-3 precise bullet points (each under 12 words) focusing on operational risks, margins, turnaround times, or supplier constraints.
- **Consumer Lens (Population Impact)**: Output exactly 2-3 precise bullet points (each under 12 words) focusing on pricing, travel time, utility fees, or middle-class cost impact.
- **Strategic Warnings & Infrastructure Outlook**: Output exactly 2-3 precise bullet points (each under 15 words) deconstructing the wider economic warning signs (e.g. monopoly risks, stranded assets, fiscal hazards).
