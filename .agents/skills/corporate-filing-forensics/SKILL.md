# Corporate Filing & Financial Footnote Forensics

When executing this track, you must perform a forensic audit of the corporate filing text. Act as a highly cynical external auditor examining the entity's financial health:

## 1. Related Party Transactions Schedule
- Scan for transactions with parent entities, subsidiaries, promoter-owned firms, and key management personnel (KMP).
- Audit whether loans, guarantees, or asset sales are conducted at "arm's length".
- Flag any massive receivables, interest-free advances, or unexplained management fees paid out to related parties.

## 2. Footnotes & Annexures
- Do not rely on the high-level Balance Sheet. Navigate directly to the footnotes explaining Contingent Liabilities, disputed tax demands, and auditor qualifications.
- Look for capital commitments that exceed the firm's cash position or cash generation capacity.
- Scrutinize lease commitments and off-balance-sheet structures.

## 3. Predatory Capital & CCPS Structures
- Examine Cumulative Convertible Preference Shares (CCPS) and deconstruct their conversion ratios and timelines.
- Look for unequal rewards where retail investors face default conversion formulas (e.g. 1 share per 6000 owned) while founders or PE funds retain highly lucrative conversion structures in hidden annexures.
- Detail the exact dilution impact on pre-IPO shareholders.

## 4. Cash Flow vs. Narrative PR Mismatches
- Compare the narrative marketing pitch (e.g., claims of high organic user base growth, low marketing burn) against audited numbers.
- Calculate marketing burn-to-revenue ratios and check if user acquisition is heavily subsidized by paid marketing rather than organic word-of-mouth.
- Scrutinize the "Restated Statement of Cash Flows" to ensure operating cash flows are positive or improving; flag any operating cash outflow paired with narrative claims of profitability.
