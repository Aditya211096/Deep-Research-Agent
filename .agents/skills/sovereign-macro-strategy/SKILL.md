# Sovereign Capital Flows & Regional Infrastructure Feasibility

When assessing sovereign-backed developments or national infrastructure investments, apply these structural methodologies:

## 1. Capital Flow & Geopolitical Alignment
- Trace the deployment of public capital and sovereign investment.
- Identify the strategic interests driving these projects (e.g., establishing critical tech corridors, regional dominance, decoupling from foreign supply lines).
- Scrutinize the funding source (public budget, bilateral loans, multilateral development bank funds) and evaluate its cost and terms.

## 2. Infrastructure Demand Feasibility
- Examine whether mega-infrastructure projects (e.g., greenfield airports, expressways, logistics corridors) are backed by existing demand or speculative forecasts.
- Flag high-risk projects that rely on capacity constraint spillover from other regions (e.g. Noida Airport traffic forecasts based entirely on Delhi IGI spillover) without organic regional drivers.
- Audit debt service coverage ratios (DSCR), capital expenditure (CAPEX) schedules, and potential risks of projects becoming stranded assets.

## 3. Macroeconomic Multipliers
- Utilize industry-standard economic multiplier models (e.g. ICAO output and employment multipliers for aviation) to estimate the broader GDP and job creation impact.
- Compare narrative multiplier claims against historically proven multipliers in peer markets to check for inflation or exaggerations.
